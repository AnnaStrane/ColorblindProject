"I Don't See Color"

What does that look like?

Normal Vision:
Uses all three types of cone
cells which are functioning
correctly. Also called
trichromacy.

Deuteranopia:
Reduced sensitivity to
green light.
(the most common form
of color blindness)

Protanopia:
Reduced sensitivity to
red light.

Tritanopia :
Reduced sensitivity to
blue light.
(The least common form
of color blindness)

Soap dispensers and self-driving cars
literally being unable to see darker skin tones.

Voice recognition software not being
trained with a full variety of accents
and vernaculars. "My dialect should be
good enough for everyone!"

Developers creating AI and deep learning computers
without including any sort of data filters or
bias recognition then taking no responsibility
for the machine learning biases.
"The internet made it racist, not us!"

Hiding behind data points that are not race itself but are historical markers of race to make business decisions. For example, using zip codes to target marketing campaigns to high income neighborhoods. This lets decision-makers claim it is not a race-based choice, while ignoring the fact that many neighborhoods in the US have distinct concentrations of incomes because of decades of intentional exclusion of non-white residents through redlining or blatant racial exclusion laws (shout out to our hometown of Portland, Oregon for having racial exclusion laws literally since its conception!).
